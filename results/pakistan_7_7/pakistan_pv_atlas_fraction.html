Where Pakistan's rooftops already carry solar

This map is not the detector above — it's a per-pixel PV-fraction regression model, trained only on Germany and never fine-tuned on Pakistan, reading the same year of Sentinel-2 imagery. Individually it barely finds small arrays; summed over a 0.1° grid, the aggregate signal survives the pixel-scale noise. That's the bet this atlas is testing: can a model that's weak at "is this one roof solar?" still be right about "how much solar is in this district?"

How to read it. Colour is expected panel area converted to peak capacity at 0.18 kWp/m², then calibrated (see below). Expected sums per-pixel PV fraction across each building footprint, with no hard cutoff; detected counts only pixels above a 0.10 fraction that also fall within 2 km of a mapped building — a much stricter, isolation-filtered floor. Cells with no signal are drawn as bare land. This is a research estimate, not a metered one.

Coverage gap. This map only covers the ~1,393 building-dense 0.1° cells the pipeline has imaged — not all of Pakistan. Matching those cells against TransitionZero's national hex grid, they hold an estimated 49% of the country's total distributed-solar capacity; the other 51% sits in cells never imaged here and is absent from every number on this page, calibrated or not.

How the capacity is estimated

The model returns a continuous PV fraction for every 10 m Sentinel-2 pixel — not a class probability, a coverage estimate, trained to be honest in aggregate rather than confident per pixel. Panel area on building roofs is converted to peak DC capacity with a single module-density constant, then summed per cell, province and country.

Aexp sums the raw per-pixel fraction with no hard cutoff — the number to trust in aggregate, before calibration. Raw nationwide (expected / detected): 4,904 / 3,888 MWp. Calibration rescales both by the same k = 2.75× to anchor the total inside our imaged cells to what TransitionZero's independent satellite + survey estimate implies is actually there in that footprint — giving the calibrated pair shown on this page: 13,480 / 10,688 MWp. That's one global scale correction, not a per-cell fix: it does not resolve the underlying spatial unreliability (the model undershoots confident hubs like Lahore and overshoots scattered rural cells via diffuse low-confidence noise), and it does not extend past the 49% of the country this pipeline has actually imaged.
