Where Pakistan's rooftops already carry solar

A recall-first segmentation model reads a year of Sentinel-2 imagery across every building-populated cell of Pakistan and marks the pixels that look like photovoltaic panels. Aggregated to each building and then to a 0.1° grid, the detected panel area becomes an estimate of installed rooftop capacity — the input an energy-system model needs. The map glows where that capacity concentrates.

How to read it. Colour is detected panel area converted to peak capacity at 0.18 kWp/m². Detected counts pixels above the 0.3 probability threshold that fall on a building footprint; expected sums probability across the footprint. Cells with no detected PV are drawn as bare land. Candidates are meant for human validation, so treat cell values as indicative, not metered.

How the capacity is estimated

The model returns a PV probability for every 10 m Sentinel-2 pixel. Panel area on building roofs is converted to peak DC capacity with a single module-density constant, then summed per cell, province and country. Two area estimates bracket the truth.

Adet counts only pixels above the 0.30 threshold that fall on a building footprint — the precision-honest floor. Aexp integrates sub-threshold probability, so it leans high. Reported capacity is P = A × η for each, giving the detected / expected pair (4,720 / 4,163 MWp nationwide) that brackets the true installed capacity.
